Career

Record-Breaking Milestones

May 2025 · Jacksonville, Florida
Ghana 100m NR — 9.86s · World Lead
At the NCAA East Prelims, Saminu obliterated Benjamin Azamati's 9.90s national record with a blazing 9.86s — becoming Ghana's fastest man ever and the fastest man on the planet in 2025. The run was just 0.04s off Christian Coleman's NCAA record and placed him joint-second in NCAA history.
July 2025 · Georgia, USA
Ghana NR Broken Again — 9.84s
Two months after his first world-leading mark, Saminu lowered the bar to 9.84s at the Last Chance Qualifier in Georgia. This time placed him joint third fastest in the world in 2025, behind only Jamaica's Kishane Thompson (9.75s) and Oblique Seville (9.83s). He became Africa's third fastest man of all time.
Paris · August 2024
Paris Olympic Games 🇫🇷
Represented Ghana at the 2024 Paris Olympics, finishing 3rd in Heat 5 of the 100m (10.06s) — qualifying automatically for the semi-finals alongside Olympic champion Marcell Jacobs. In the semis, he lined up against Fred Kerley, Kishane Thompson, Zharnel Hughes and Ferdinand Omanyala. Also ran in Ghana's 4×100m relay.
Tokyo · September 2025
World Athletics Championships 🌏
Advanced to the 100m semi-finals in Tokyo. Anchored Ghana's 4×100m relay team to a 4th place finish and a new Ghanaian relay national record of 37.79 seconds — a historic performance for West African sprinting. Named Male Athlete of the Year at the Stampede of Champions Awards 2025.
Douala · 2024
African Athletics Gold 🥇
Won gold in the 4×100m relay at the African Athletics Championships in Douala, Cameroon. Also swept the AAC Championships in San Antonio with gold medals in the 100m, 200m and 4×100m relay. Named USF's Male Athlete of the Year and First Team All-American in the 60m indoor.
January 2026
ASICS Professional Contract ✍️
Signed a landmark 3-year professional endorsement deal with ASICS (Oct 2025 – Dec 2028, option for 2029) covering travel, physical therapy, and coaching. He continues working with his college coach Abigi Id-Deen. Eyes set on the 2027 World Championships and the Los Angeles 2028 Olympic Games.

About

🇬🇭 Nanumba · Northern Region, Ghana

The Village Boy
Who Flew

Abdul-Rasheed Saminu was born on 6 October 1997 in the village of Kukuo, Nanumba, in Ghana's Northern Region — raised by his grandparents from age six while his parents worked across the border in Nigeria. Sport was survival first, football came second. "At that time, I wasn't dreaming about going to the Olympics. I didn't even know what that was," he has said. "I was thinking about the next meal."

His extraordinary speed caught coaches' eyes and he pivoted from football to athletics — earning the nickname "Gatlin" as a schoolboy sprinter at Al Azhariya Islamic Senior High School. He attended the University of Ghana, then Florida Memorial University, before being recruited to the University of South Florida (USF) by head coach Erik Jenkins. At USF, he became one of the most decorated sprinters in NCAA history — and earned a Bachelor of Science in Communication.

In May 2025, Saminu electrified the world with 9.86s at the NCAA East Prelims — a world lead and new Ghanaian record. Two months later in Georgia he ran 9.84s, making him the third-fastest African of all time. A Paris Olympian, a Tokyo World Champion semi-finalist, and now an ASICS professional athlete — his story is Ghana's story of belief made real at 100 metres per everything.
